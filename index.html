01 — About

Beyond pipeline orchestration: constructing theories

AI agents for scientific discovery are proliferating, but they share a fundamental limitation: they treat science as a pipeline-selection problem. Current AI scientists receive textual experiment descriptions, search the literature, and orchestrate pre-existing tools—reducing scientific reasoning to text matching.

Yet science is the ability to construct a model from data, generate predictions from that model, and validate those predictions through experiments. This workshop asks: how do we build AI scientists that construct, simulate, and revise mechanistic models of the world?

The next generation of AI scientists should infer generative models of complex systems—"virtual cells," "virtual brains," "virtual organisms"—whose behavior can be simulated, perturbed, and interrogated. These models encode explicit hypotheses about how components interact and evolve, forming a closed loop: model → prediction → experiment → revision.

Generative models (neural ODEs/SDEs, flow matching)
Virtual cells, brains & organisms
Lab-in-the-loop experimental design
Geometric & topological deep learning
AI agents for model construction
Cross-domain generalizability
Evaluation & benchmarks for AI scientists
Hypothesis generation & model falsification
Language reasoning + geometric models

04 — Call for Papers

Submit your work

We welcome contributions across all workshop topics. Submissions via OpenReview.

Full Papers

4–8 pages

Original research contributions with methods, experiments, and comparisons. Top papers selected for spotlight talks and awards.

Extended Abstracts

4 pages

Work-in-progress results, preliminary findings, or position papers framing new directions.

Tiny Papers

1–2 pages

Novel methodological ideas or approaches to biological problems. Designed to encourage participation from under-represented communities. Lack of results is not grounds for rejection.
